Data Retention

Effective date: 18/05/2026

BillShield retains user data only for as long as needed for the purpose for which it was collected, for account servicing, for dispute continuity, for security and fraud prevention, and to comply with legal obligations. India’s DPDP Act requires organizations not to retain personal data longer than necessary unless retention is required by law.

1. Retention categories

We may retain the following for different periods:

  • Account data: retained while your account remains active and for a reasonable period afterward for support, auditability, and reactivation.
  • Uploaded documents: retained while needed to provide analysis, preserve issue history, or support user-requested dispute/claims workflows.
  • Generated outputs: retained alongside source documents so you can revisit prior analyses.
  • System and security logs: retained in line with operational necessity and applicable cybersecurity directions. CERT-In requires certain ICT logs to be maintained securely for a rolling period of 180 days.
  • Backups: may persist for a limited recovery period even after deletion requests, after which they are overwritten in the normal backup cycle.

2. Deletion and user requests

You may request deletion of your account data or uploaded files, subject to:

  • legal retention obligations,
  • fraud-prevention and abuse-review needs,
  • preservation of records needed to establish, exercise, or defend legal claims,
  • limited backup and disaster-recovery cycles.

Where deletion is approved, active-use data will be removed or de-identified within a reasonable period.

3. When we may retain data longer

We may retain limited data longer where required:

  • to comply with tax, accounting, contractual, or legal obligations;
  • to respond to law-enforcement or regulatory requests;
  • to investigate misuse, security incidents, or fraud;
  • to preserve evidence relating to disputes, insurer interactions, or chargeback issues.cert-in+1

4. Security during retention

Data that is retained remains subject to our access controls and security safeguards. Retention does not mean unrestricted access.

5. Inactive accounts

If your account becomes inactive and there is no ongoing service need, we may delete or anonymize associated documents after a defined inactivity period, unless retention is required by law or necessary to protect our legitimate legal interests.
