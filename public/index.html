Why hospital bills in India are a minefield

Problem 1: No Itemization

The Issue: Hospitals bundle charges into vague line items like "ICU Consumables ₹85,000" or "Surgical Package ₹1,50,000" with zero breakdown.

What You Can't See: Whether you were charged for supplies you never received, procedures that weren't performed, or equipment that was reused but billed as new.

BillShield Finds: Missing itemization, unexplained bundles, and charges that don't match your discharge summary.

Problem 2: Markup Madness

The Issue: Medicines sold at 250-400% above Maximum Retail Price. Diagnostic tests charged 8-10x the CGHS benchmark. No justification required.

Examples:
• Paracetamol injection (MRP ₹12) billed at ₹65
• CT scan (CGHS rate ₹2,500) billed at ₹19,000

BillShield Finds: Every medicine above MRP (which is illegal), every procedure above government benchmarks, every device above NPPA caps.

Problem 3: Insurance Rejection Tricks

The Issue: Insurers reject valid claims hoping you won't appeal. They cite vague policy clauses, miss their own settlement deadlines, or bury you in paperwork.

What They're Banking On: That you don't know IRDAI made most rejections illegal in 2026. That you won't file complaints. That you'll give up.

BillShield Finds: Regulatory violations, wrongful rejections, and gives you pre-filled IRDAI complaint forms with exact regulation numbers cited.

The 6 types of overcharges we fight

1. Duplicate Charges

Same procedure code appears twice. Consultation fee billed on multiple days when discharge summary shows single visit. Billing software errors or deliberate padding.

2. Above-MRP Medicine Billing

Paracetamol billed at ₹45 when MRP is ₹12. Medicines marked up 300-400%. Consumer Protection Act prohibits selling medicines above printed MRP.

3. Above-Benchmark Procedures

CT scan charged ₹18,000 vs ₹2,500 CGHS rate. Specialist consultation ₹5,000 vs ₹1,000 CGHS rate. 8-10x markups with no justification.

4. Phantom Charges

Bill lists 8 specialist consultations. Discharge summary names 2 specialists. ICU stay billed for 4 days when patient records show 2.

5. Mysterious Bundled Fees

"ICU Consumables ₹95,000" with zero breakdown. "Surgical Package ₹2,40,000" with no itemization. You can't verify what you're paying for.

6. Insurance Regulation Violations

Claim rejected 28 days after submission (IRDAI rule: must approve/reject within 15 days). Claim rejected for "non-payable item" not in policy exclusions.

Is my information secure? ▼
Yes. We use bank-level encryption (AES-256). We strip personal identifiers before storing bills. Documents are auto-deleted after 30 days unless you opt into anonymized data sharing.
How long does it take? ▼
Analysis takes 8-15 minutes on average. You'll receive an email and SMS when your report is ready. If you're standing at the billing counter, our summary page prioritizes the top 3 most actionable flags so you can act immediately.
What if the hospital or insurer ignores my report? ▼
For hospitals: Our escalation path includes requesting itemized breakdowns (required under Consumer Protection Act), filing complaints with State Medical Council. For insurers: Our IRDAI complaint template goes directly to Insurance Ombudsman. Insurers MUST respond to IRDAI complaints within mandated timelines or face penalties.
