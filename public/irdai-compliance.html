IRDAI Compliance

BillShield is built to support Indian patients and policyholders navigating medical bills and insurance workflows. We are not regulated by IRDAI as an insurer, insurance intermediary, or TPA unless expressly stated otherwise. However, our user experience and support flows are designed to align with Indian policyholder-protection expectations where relevant.

1. Consumer-facing positioning

IRDAI’s 2024 Master Circular on Protection of Policyholders’ Interests emphasizes clear communication, grievance redressal, claim servicing standards, and accessible customer information for policyholders. BillShield aims to support those objectives by helping users organize claim documents, understand billing components, and prepare more informed hospital or insurer queries.

2. What BillShield does not do

BillShield does not:

  • issue or underwrite insurance;
  • settle claims;
  • bind coverage;
  • act as an insurer’s grievance portal;
  • replace the policy document, Customer Information Sheet, or insurer claims process required under IRDAI rules.

3. Grievance awareness

Where users face unresolved insurance claim or servicing issues, they may also use insurer grievance channels and IRDAI’s Bima Bharosa platform. IRDAI’s circular states that complaints can be registered with the insurer and also through Bima Bharosa, and unresolved or partially resolved complaints up to the prescribed monetary threshold may be escalated to the Insurance Ombudsman.

Suggested consumer pathway:

  1. Raise the complaint with the insurer or TPA.
  2. Preserve acknowledgment, claim number, and supporting documents.
  3. Escalate through Bima Bharosa if unresolved.
  4. Approach the Insurance Ombudsman where eligible.

4. Claim-support disclaimers

BillShield may help you identify:

  • missing itemization,
  • possible benchmark mismatches,
  • unclear package charges,
  • documentation gaps,
  • insurer communication issues.

But final claim admissibility depends on the insurance contract, underwriting history, exclusions, waiting periods, room-rent sub-limits, package rules, and insurer decisioning.

5. No representation of IRDAI approval

Use of the term “IRDAI Compliance” on this page means our effort to align user information practices with Indian insurance-consumer norms where applicable. It does not mean BillShield is approved, endorsed, licensed, or certified by IRDAI unless separately and expressly stated.

6. Ombudsman and timelines

IRDAI’s 2024 circular states that insurers must resolve complaints within 14 days and that unresolved or partially resolved eligible complaints may be taken to the Insurance Ombudsman, generally for claim values up to Rs. 50 lakhs. The same circular also describes consumer access through Bima Bharosa and ombudsman routes.

7. Practical use of BillShield with insurance complaints

Use BillShield outputs as supporting material, not as a substitute for:

  • policy wording,
  • discharge summary,
  • final bill,
  • itemized invoices,
  • prescriptions,
  • claim forms,
  • TPA queries,
  • denial letters.
