Privacy Policy

Effective date: 18/05/2026

BillShield helps users upload, organize, and review medical billing documents such as hospital bills, pharmacy invoices, discharge summaries, lab reports, and claim-related documents. In doing so, we may process personal data and sensitive health-related information contained in those records. Our goal is to use that information only for lawful, limited, and user-facing purposes such as document analysis, issue detection, dispute preparation, and claim-support workflows 

1. What we collect

We may collect:

  • Account details such as your name, email address, phone number, and login identifiers.
  • Uploaded documents such as bills, prescriptions, discharge summaries, insurer letters, lab reports, and supporting claim files.
  • Information extracted from those documents, including patient name, age, admission dates, hospital name, billed items, amounts, insurer references, and medical treatment details where present.
  • Technical information such as IP address, browser/device metadata, access timestamps, and system logs needed for security, fraud prevention, debugging, and platform reliability.

2. Why we use your data

We use your data to:

  • provide bill analysis and issue identification;
  • improve document classification, extraction, and benchmark workflows;
  • generate summaries, challenge letters, and claim-support outputs requested by you;
  • maintain account security, prevent abuse, and investigate technical incidents;
  • comply with applicable legal obligations.

We do not sell your personal data.

3. Legal basis and consent

Where required, we rely on your consent to process personal data you submit to us. Under India’s Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023, consent must be free, specific, informed, unconditional, and unambiguous, and users must be able to withdraw it with comparable ease. We design our workflows with that principle in mind.

If you upload records relating to another person, you are responsible for ensuring you are authorized to do so.

4. Health and financial information

Medical bills and insurance records can reveal diagnoses, treatment patterns, admission history, and claim status. We treat such information as high-sensitivity data and apply access controls, encryption in transit, and restricted internal access on a need-to-know basis.

BillShield is a document-analysis and consumer-support product, not a hospital, insurer, TPA, or emergency medical service. Our outputs are informational and should be reviewed before being submitted as complaints, legal notices, or insurer-facing representations.

5. Sharing of data

We may share data only with:

  • service providers who help us host, store, secure, or process the platform;
  • AI or OCR vendors used to analyze uploaded documents;
  • legal, regulatory, or law-enforcement authorities where required by law;
  • parties you explicitly instruct us to share with, such as your insurer, hospital, advisor, or family member.

We require service providers to process data only for authorized purposes and under contractual controls.

6. Your rights

Subject to applicable law, you may request:

  • access to the personal data we hold about you;
  • correction or updating of inaccurate data;
  • deletion of data where retention is no longer necessary or consent is withdrawn, unless retention is required by law;
  • grievance redressal through our support contact.

Under the DPDP Act, users also have a right to grievance redressal and a right to nominate another person to exercise rights in the event of death or incapacity.

7. Security

We implement reasonable technical and organizational safeguards to protect data against unauthorized access, disclosure, loss, or misuse. We also maintain certain ICT/system logs for security operations, incident investigation, and regulatory readiness, consistent with CERT-In’s directions requiring secure log retention for a rolling period of 180 days.

No system can guarantee absolute security, but we aim to minimize exposure by limiting access, retaining data carefully, and reviewing vendor security practices.

8. Children’s data

Our service is not intended for use by children acting on their own behalf. If personal data relating to a child is uploaded, it should be done only by a parent, guardian, or other authorized adult. India’s DPDP Act imposes special rules on processing children’s personal data.

9. Data retention

We retain data only for as long as needed for the purpose for which it was collected, for user-requested workflows, for dispute history, for platform integrity, or to comply with law. More detail is available on our Data Retention page.cert-in+1
